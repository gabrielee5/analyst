# Domain Profile

## Classification
- **Domain**: Financial Analysis (Historical Corporate)
- **Sub-domain**: Corporate history, industrial economics, and long-cycle financial analysis
- **Analyst Persona**: Senior equity research analyst specializing in industrial/materials sector with deep historical expertise — the kind of analyst who writes definitive "rise and fall" corporate histories for institutional investors
- **Report Type**: Comprehensive Corporate History & Financial Analysis

## Report Structure
1. Executive Summary — The Arc of Bethlehem Steel
2. Company Timeline — Detailed Chronological Milestones (1857–2003)
3. Founding and Early History (1857–1904) — From Iron to Steel
4. The Charles Schwab Era and Rise to Dominance (1904–1939)
5. World War I: Arsenal of Democracy's Forge
6. Interwar Period: Expansion, Labor, and the Great Depression
7. World War II: Peak Industrial Output
8. Post-War Golden Age (1945–1970) — Infrastructure Boom and Market Dominance
9. Decline and Structural Challenges (1970–1995) — Foreign Competition, Labor Costs, and Mini-Mills
10. Final Chapter: Bankruptcy and Liquidation (1995–2003)
11. Financial Performance Across Eras — Revenue, Margins, and Capital Returns
12. Stock Price History and Shareholder Value Analysis
13. Legacy and Lessons — What Bethlehem Steel Teaches About Industrial Economics

## Deep-Dive Instructions
- Search for SEC filings, annual reports, and historical financial data from Bethlehem Steel Corporation
- Look for historical stock price data across different eras (pre-war, post-war boom, decline)
- Research labor relations history: strikes, union contracts, pension obligations, retiree healthcare costs
- Investigate the competitive landscape: US Steel, foreign steelmakers (Japan, Korea), mini-mill disruptors (Nucor)
- Find production volume data: tons of steel shipped, capacity utilization rates across decades
- Search for major capital projects: shipbuilding, bridges, skyscrapers, military contracts
- Research the bankruptcy proceedings (2001–2003) and asset disposition
- Look for executive leadership transitions and strategic decisions at inflection points
- Find data on the Bethlehem PA plant and community impact
- Search academic and business case studies analyzing the company's decline

## Source Priorities
1. SEC filings, annual reports, and proxy statements (later years)
2. Academic business history papers and case studies
3. Historical financial databases and stock price records
4. Industry publications (Iron Age, American Metal Market, Steel magazine)
5. Business press coverage (Wall Street Journal, NYT, Fortune, Forbes)
6. Labor history archives and union records
7. Government reports (trade commission, congressional hearings on steel tariffs)
8. Books on Bethlehem Steel (e.g., "Making Steel" by Mark Reutter)
9. Lehigh Valley historical society records

## Review Criteria
- Does the timeline include all major inflection points with specific dates?
- Are financial figures cited with time periods and adjusted for context (inflation where relevant)?
- Is the competitive analysis thorough — covering domestic rivals, imports, and mini-mills?
- Are labor/pension cost dynamics quantified, not just mentioned?
- Does the analysis explain *why* the company failed, not just *that* it failed?
- Are stock price movements contextualized against industry and macro events?
- Is the narrative balanced — acknowledging both management failures and structural/macro headwinds?

## Brief Sections
- Company Name & Identifiers (ticker, exchange, SIC code)
- Analysis Scope (full corporate lifecycle: 1857–2003)
- Core Analytical Question (how and why did America's second-largest steelmaker rise and fall?)
- Key Metrics to Track (revenue, production volume, employment, stock price, debt levels, pension obligations)
- Peer Group (US Steel, Nucor, National Steel, LTV Steel, Inland Steel)
- Time Periods of Interest (founding, WWI, WWII, post-war boom, 1970s crisis, bankruptcy)
- Deliverables (complete timeline, financial charts across eras, evolution prospectus)

## Writing Style
Data-heavy, narrative-driven financial history. Lead with the investment thesis of each era — why was Bethlehem Steel a buy, hold, or sell at each inflection point? Use concrete financial data wherever available. Write with the authority of a senior analyst who has studied this company for decades. Balance quantitative rigor with compelling narrative — this is a story as much as an analysis. No emojis. Clean, precise prose.
