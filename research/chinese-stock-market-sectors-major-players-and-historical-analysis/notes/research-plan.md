# Research Plan: Chinese Stock Market

## Core Research Question
What is the structural landscape of China's equity markets in 2026 — how are they organized by sector, who are the dominant players, and how did a century of political and economic upheaval shape the market that exists today?

## Sub-Questions
1. How did China's stock markets originate, die, and get reborn?
2. What is the modern exchange architecture and how do share classes / access channels work?
3. What are the current sector weights and how have they evolved?
4. Who are the largest companies in each major sector?
5. What were the key index price movements and returns over the full modern history (1990–2026)?
6. What were the causes, mechanics, and aftermath of each major market crash/rally?
7. How has foreign access evolved and what role do Stock Connect flows play?
8. What is the current valuation, policy, and outlook picture?

## Subtopics for Deep Dives

### 1. Historical Origins & Pre-Modern Era (1860s–1990)
- Shanghai's first stock exchanges (1860s–1940s)
- Closure under Mao, planned economy era
- Deng Xiaoping's reforms and the 1990 reopening
- **Sources**: Academic histories, SSE historical archives, news retrospectives

### 2. Market Architecture & Regulatory Framework
- SSE, SZSE, BSE, HKEX structure and listing requirements
- A/B/H-share classes, Red chips, VIE structures
- CSRC regulatory evolution, registration-based IPO reform
- Circuit breakers, T+1 settlement, short-selling rules
- **Sources**: Exchange fact books, CSRC publications, regulatory analyses

### 3. Sector Landscape & Weight Evolution
- Current GICS sector breakdown of CSI 300, CSI 500, Hang Seng
- How sector weights shifted over time (financials dominance → tech rise → new energy)
- Sector-level P/E ratios and performance
- **Sources**: Index providers (CSI, Hang Seng), Bloomberg, Wind data

### 4. Major Players — Financials, Energy & Industrials
- Top banks (ICBC, CCB, ABC, BOC), insurance (Ping An, China Life), brokers (CITIC, Huatai)
- Energy giants (PetroChina, Sinopec, CNOOC, Yangtze Power, LONGi)
- Industrial leaders (CRRC, Sany, CATL crossover)
- Market caps, revenue, and competitive positioning
- **Sources**: Company filings, Bloomberg, analyst reports

### 5. Major Players — Technology, Consumer & Healthcare
- Tech/internet: Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan, PDD, Baidu, JD.com, ByteDance (unlisted)
- Semiconductors: SMIC, Huawei HiSilicon, NAURA, Cambricon
- Consumer discretionary: BYD, Li Auto, NIO, Anta Sports, Haidilao
- Consumer staples: Kweichow Moutai, Wuliangye, Yili, Mengniu
- Healthcare: WuXi AppTec, Hengrui, Mindray, Jiangsu Hengrui
- **Sources**: Company filings, analyst reports, news coverage

### 6. Index Performance & Price History (1990–2026)
- SSE Composite full history: key levels, returns by decade
- CSI 300 from 2005 inception
- Hang Seng and HSCEI performance
- ChiNext and STAR Market since launch
- Comparison with S&P 500, Nikkei, MSCI EM
- **Sources**: Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, exchange data, financial news archives

### 7. Major Market Events & Crises (2005–2026)
- 2005–2007 split-share reform bull run (SSE 1000 → 6124)
- 2008 GFC crash
- 2014–2015 leveraged bubble and crash, circuit breaker fiasco
- 2018 trade war selloff
- 2020 COVID crash and recovery
- 2021 tech crackdown (Ant IPO, education, gaming)
- 2022–2023 property crisis and COVID lockdown malaise
- 2024 September stimulus bazooka and rally
- 2025 DeepSeek AI rally and Hang Seng Tech surge
- **Sources**: Financial journalism (FT, Bloomberg, Caixin, Reuters), CSRC records

### 8. Foreign Access, Capital Flows & Internationalization
- QFII/RQFII program history and quotas
- Stock Connect launch and evolution (northbound/southbound flows)
- MSCI A-share inclusion journey (2018 partial → current weight)
- Foreign ownership levels and trends
- US-listed Chinese ADRs and delisting risks
- **Sources**: HKEX Stock Connect data, MSCI publications, SEC filings, news

## Methodology
- Combine historical narrative with quantitative data
- Source all index levels and dates from verifiable financial data
- Use sell-side research framing but maintain analytical independence
- Present risks and bull cases with equal rigor

## Expected Output
~4,000–5,500 word report following the domain profile's 10-section structure, with 6-8 data charts, 30+ footnoted sources, and comprehensive sector/company tables.
