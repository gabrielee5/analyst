# Index-Level Performance and Price History of China's Stock Markets (1990--2026)

## Summary

China's stock markets have delivered extraordinary volatility since inception, with boom-bust cycles far more extreme than developed market peers. The SSE Composite Index, launched in December 1990 at a base of 100, has produced a cumulative return of roughly 3,850% to its current level near 3,949 (April 2026), but this headline figure masks violent drawdowns: -65% in 2008, -49% from peak to trough in 2015, and a prolonged bear market from 2021 to early 2024. The Hang Seng Index, Hong Kong's benchmark with data stretching to 1964, peaked at 31,958 in October 2007 and spent nearly two decades failing to durably surpass that level, only recently recovering to ~26,797 in April 2026 after touching a 13-year low of 14,597 in October 2022. The CSI 300, the most institutionally tracked A-share benchmark, has underperformed the S&P 500 significantly since its 2005 launch on a cumulative basis. MSCI China, which includes offshore listings, has been one of the worst-performing major country indices over the past decade, with annualized 10-year returns of roughly 7% in USD versus double digits for developed markets.

The core finding is that China's equity markets have been structurally poor long-term investments despite episodic spectacular rallies, largely due to the severity of drawdowns, regulatory unpredictability, and the dilutive effect of massive new issuance.

### Total Return vs. Price Return

The CSI 300 is a price-return index. The CSI 300 Total Return Index includes reinvested dividends. Historically, A-share dividend yields have averaged 1.5--2.5% per annum, meaning the total return index outperforms the price index by a cumulative 30--50% over a 15-year period. As of recent data, the CSI 300 Total Return Index has roughly a 35--40% cumulative advantage over the price-only index since 2005.

### CSI 300 vs. S&P 500 (2005--2025)

| Metric | CSI 300 (Price) | S&P 500 (Price) | S&P 500 (Total Return) |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| End-2005 level (rebased to 1000) | 1,000 | 1,000 | 1,000 |
| End-2025 level (approx.) | ~4,873 | ~5,200 | ~7,100 |
| Annualized return (20 yrs) | ~8.2% | ~8.6% | ~10.3% |

The CSI 300 narrowly trails the S&P 500 on a price-return basis over the full period, but significantly underperforms on a total-return basis due to the S&P 500's higher and more consistent dividend reinvestment. Moreover, the CSI 300's path was far more volatile: it was up 161% in 2007, then down 66% in 2008. The S&P 500's worst year in the same period was -38% (2008).
