# Research Brief: Chinese Stock Market — Sectors, Major Players & Historical Analysis

## Market Scope
- **Exchanges**: Shanghai Stock Exchange (SSE), Shenzhen Stock Exchange (SZSE), Beijing Stock Exchange (BSE), Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKEX)
- **Share classes**: A-shares (RMB-denominated, domestic), B-shares (USD/HKD-denominated), H-shares (Hong Kong-listed mainland companies), Red chips, P-chips, S-chips
- **Key indices**: SSE Composite, CSI 300, CSI 500, ChiNext, STAR Market 50, Hang Seng Index, Hang Seng China Enterprises Index (HSCEI), MSCI China
- **Scope**: Mainland China equity markets as the primary focus, with Hong Kong as a critical access point and listing venue

## Analysis Period
- **Full span**: 1860s–2026 (Shanghai's first stock exchange activity through to present day)
- **Pre-modern era** (1860s–1949): Light coverage — origins, early exchanges, wartime disruption
- **Dormancy** (1949–1990): Brief treatment — planned economy, no public markets
- **Modern era** (1990–present): Core focus — reopening of exchanges through current state
- **Emphasis**: Heaviest coverage on 2000–2026, with detailed treatment of each major market event

## Core Research Question
What is the structural landscape of China's equity markets today — how are they organized by sector, who are the dominant players, and how did a century of political and economic upheaval shape the market that exists in 2026?

## Key Metrics to Include
- Total market capitalization (A-shares, Hong Kong-listed Chinese companies)
- Index levels at key historical dates (with % drawdowns for crashes)
- Sector weights in CSI 300 and Hang Seng
- Top 10 companies by market cap (overall and per major sector)
- P/E ratios (current and historical range)
- Average daily trading volume / turnover
- Number of listed companies over time
- Foreign ownership % and Stock Connect flow data

## Sector Coverage
All major GICS sectors represented in Chinese equities:
- **Financials** (banks, insurance, brokerages)
- **Technology / Communication Services** (internet platforms, semiconductors, software)
- **Consumer Discretionary** (e-commerce, autos/EVs, luxury, travel)
- **Consumer Staples** (baijiu/liquor, food & beverage, dairy)
- **Industrials** (construction, machinery, defense, logistics)
- **Energy** (oil & gas, coal, renewables/solar/wind)
- **Materials** (steel, chemicals, rare earths, lithium)
- **Healthcare** (pharma, biotech, medical devices, CROs)
- **Real Estate** (developers, property services — and the ongoing crisis)
- **Utilities** (power generation, water, nuclear)

## Historical Events to Address
1. Pre-1849 Shanghai Bourse and its closure
2. 1990: Reopening of SSE and SZSE
3. 2001: WTO accession and market liberalization
4. 2005–2007: Split-share reform and the great bull run
5. 2007–2008: Bubble burst and global financial crisis
6. 2014: Shanghai-Hong Kong Stock Connect launch
7. 2015: Bubble and crash (leveraged margin trading crisis)
8. 2018–2019: US-China trade war impact
9. 2020: COVID crash and V-shaped recovery
10. 2020–2021: Ant Group IPO cancellation and tech regulatory crackdown
11. 2022–2023: Property crisis, COVID lockdowns, market malaise
12. 2024–2025: Stimulus pivot, DeepSeek AI rally, policy shift
13. 2025–2026: Current state and outlook

- India (Nifty 50) as an emerging market peer
- Japan (Nikkei) for historical parallels (bubble, lost decade narratives)
