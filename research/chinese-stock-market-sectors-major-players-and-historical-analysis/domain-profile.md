# Domain Profile

## Classification
- **Domain**: Financial / Market Analysis (Hybrid with Historical Analysis)
- **Sub-domain**: China Equity Markets — Structural Overview, Sector Mapping & Historical Evolution
- **Analyst Persona**: Senior China equities market analyst with deep knowledge of mainland and Hong Kong exchanges, regulatory history, and cross-border capital flows
- **Report Type**: Market Overview & Historical Analysis

## Report Structure
1. Executive Summary
2. A Century of Chinese Capital Markets — Historical Timeline (pre-1949 Shanghai Bourse through Cultural Revolution dormancy to 1990 reopening)
3. Modern Market Architecture — Exchange Structure, Share Classes & Regulatory Framework (SSE, SZSE, BSE, HKEX, A/B/H-shares, QFII/Stock Connect)
4. Sector Landscape — Mapping China's Equity Universe (sector weights in CSI 300 / Hang Seng, sectoral evolution over time)
5. Major Players by Sector — Company Profiles & Competitive Positioning (financials, technology, consumer, energy, industrials, healthcare, materials)
6. Index Performance & Price History — Long-Run Returns and Volatility (SSE Composite, CSI 300, Hang Seng, MSCI China, ChiNext)
7. Landmark Events & Market Shocks — Catalysts That Shaped the Market (2007 bubble, 2015 crash, trade war, COVID, tech crackdown, 2024-25 stimulus rally)
8. Foreign Access & Capital Flows — Internationalization of China's Markets (Stock Connect volumes, MSCI inclusion, QFII/RQFII evolution)
9. Current State & Outlook — Valuations, Policy Direction & Risks
10. Appendix — Data Tables & Methodology Notes

## Deep-Dive Instructions
Sub-agents should:
- Search for historical Shanghai Stock Exchange data pre-1949 and the 1990 reopening context.
- Pull index-level price data for SSE Composite, CSI 300, Hang Seng Index, and ChiNext from financial data providers and news archives.
- Identify the top 10 companies by market cap in each major GICS sector on the mainland exchanges and HKEX.
- Research each major market event (2007 bubble, 2008 GFC, 2015 crash, 2018 trade war, 2020 COVID, 2021 tech crackdown, 2024 stimulus) with specific dates, index levels, policy actions, and aftermath.
- Look for CSRC regulatory milestones: QFII launch (2002), Stock Connect (2014), registration-based IPO reform, short-selling rules.
- Gather sector weight breakdowns from CSI 300 and Hang Seng index methodology documents.
- Search for foreign investor positioning data (Stock Connect northbound/southbound flows, MSCI weight changes).
- Use Bloomberg, Reuters, SCMP, Caixin, Wind data references, CSRC publications, HKEX fact books.

## Source Priorities
1. Exchange publications (SSE, SZSE, HKEX fact books and annual reports)
2. Regulatory filings and CSRC announcements
3. Financial data providers (Bloomberg, Wind, Reuters)
4. Sell-side research (Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, UBS, CICC research notes)
5. Financial journalism (Caixin, SCMP, Financial Times, Reuters, Bloomberg News)
6. Academic studies on Chinese capital markets
7. IMF/World Bank reports on China's financial system

## Review Criteria
- Do index levels and dates cited match verifiable historical records?
- Are sector weights sourced and internally consistent?
- Are company market caps and rankings current (or dated if historical)?
- Does the historical timeline maintain factual accuracy for pre-1949 and Mao-era periods?
- Are regulatory milestones (QFII, Stock Connect, etc.) dated correctly?
- Is the report balanced between bullish structural narratives and genuine risks (capital controls, VIE structures, delisting risk, geopolitics)?

## Brief Sections
- Market Scope (which exchanges, indices, and share classes are covered)
- Analysis Period (1920s–2026, with emphasis on 1990–present)
- Core Research Question
- Key Metrics to Include (market cap, index levels, sector weights, P/E ratios, trading volumes)
- Sector Coverage
- Historical Events to Address
- Peer/Comparative Context (vs. other major markets where relevant)

## Writing Style
Data-heavy, structured, with clear section hierarchy. Lead each section with the key takeaway. Use tables extensively for sector breakdowns and company rankings. Maintain analytical objectivity — present both the China growth thesis and the structural risks without advocacy. Use precise dates and index levels for all market events. Adopt a professional sell-side research tone.
